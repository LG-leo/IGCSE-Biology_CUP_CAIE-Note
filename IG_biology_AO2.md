# IGCSE Biology 0610 – Keywords & Examination Points

## Single Examination Points (Topic by Topic)

| Topic | Key Single Examination Points (Keywords) |
|-------|-------------------------------------------|
| 1 Characteristics of living organisms | movement, respiration, sensitivity, growth, reproduction, excretion, nutrition |
| 2 Organisation of the organism | cell wall, cell membrane, nucleus, cytoplasm, chloroplasts, mitochondria, ribosomes, vacuole, bacterial cell (circular DNA, plasmids) |
| 3 Movement into and out of cells | diffusion (net movement down concentration gradient), osmosis (water through partially permeable membrane), active transport (against gradient, requires energy) |
| 4 Biological molecules | starch (iodine test), reducing sugars (Benedict’s test), proteins (biuret test), fats (ethanol emulsion test), vitamin C (DCPIP test); elements in carbohydrates, fats, proteins; glucose, amino acids, fatty acids & glycerol; DNA double helix, base pairing (A-T, C-G) |
| 5 Enzymes | biological catalysts, active site, substrate, enzyme-substrate complex, denaturation (temperature/pH), optimum conditions |
| 6 Plant nutrition | photosynthesis: CO₂ + H₂O → glucose + O₂ (light, chlorophyll); chlorophyll, limiting factors (light, CO₂, temperature); nitrate ions (amino acids), magnesium ions (chlorophyll) |
| 7 Human nutrition | balanced diet; sources and importance of carbohydrates, fats, proteins, vitamins (C, D), minerals (Ca, Fe), fibre, water; digestion (amylase, protease, lipase), absorption (villi), assimilation, egestion |
| 8 Transport in plants | xylem (water, mineral ions, support), phloem (sucrose, amino acids, translocation), root hair cells (large surface area), transpiration (water loss from leaves), transpiration pull, wilting |
| 9 Transport in animals | circulatory system (heart, blood vessels, valves); heart structures (atria, ventricles, septum, valves, coronary arteries); double circulation; arteries, veins, capillaries; blood components (red blood cells, white blood cells, platelets, plasma); clotting (fibrinogen → fibrin) |
| 10 Diseases and immunity | pathogen, transmissible disease, transmission (direct/indirect), body defences (skin, mucus, stomach acid, white blood cells), active immunity (antibodies), vaccination, passive immunity (breast milk, placenta) |
| 11 Gas exchange in humans | gas exchange surfaces (large SA, thin, good blood supply, ventilation); breathing system (lungs, diaphragm, ribs, intercostal muscles, trachea, bronchi, bronchioles, alveoli); inspired vs expired air (O₂, CO₂, H₂O); limewater test; ventilation mechanism (inhalation/exhalation) |
| 12 Respiration | aerobic respiration (glucose + O₂ → CO₂ + H₂O + energy); anaerobic respiration (yeast: glucose → alcohol + CO₂; muscles: glucose → lactic acid); oxygen debt; uses of energy (muscle contraction, protein synthesis, cell division, active transport, growth, nerve impulses, constant temperature) |
| 13 Excretion in humans | CO₂ (lungs), urea and excess water/ions (kidneys); kidney structure (cortex, medulla, ureter, bladder, urethra); nephron (filtration, reabsorption of glucose/water/ions, urine formation); liver (deamination, urea formation) |
| 14 Coordination and response | nervous system (CNS, PNS, sensory/relay/motor neurones), reflex arc (receptor → sensory → relay → motor → effector), synapse (neurotransmitter, one-way transmission); eye (cornea, iris, pupil, lens, retina, optic nerve, blind spot, fovea), pupil reflex, accommodation (ciliary muscles, suspensory ligaments), rods & cones; hormones (adrenaline, insulin, glucagon, testosterone, oestrogen), homeostasis (negative feedback, blood glucose regulation, temperature regulation); tropisms (phototropism, gravitropism, auxin) |
| 15 Drugs | antibiotic (bacterial infections, not viruses), antibiotic resistance (natural selection), MRSA |
| 16 Reproduction | asexual (genetically identical offspring); sexual reproduction (fusion of gametes, zygote, variation); plant reproduction (insect/wind pollinated flowers, pollination, fertilisation, seed germination); human reproduction (male/female reproductive systems, sperm/egg adaptations, fertilisation, placenta, umbilical cord, amniotic fluid); hormones (testosterone, oestrogen, progesterone, FSH, LH, menstrual cycle); STIs (HIV, transmission, control) |
| 17 Inheritance | chromosomes (DNA, genes), allele, genotype, phenotype, homozygous, heterozygous, dominant, recessive, monohybrid crosses, Punnett square, pedigree diagrams, sex chromosomes (XX, XY), sex linkage (red-green colour blindness), codominance (ABO blood groups) |
| 18 Variation and selection | continuous variation (range, genes+environment), discontinuous variation (categories, genes only), mutation (new alleles, causes: radiation, chemicals), natural selection (variation, overproduction, struggle for survival, advantageous alleles passed on), selective breeding, adaptation |
| 19 Organisms and their environment | food chains, food webs, pyramids of numbers/biomass/energy, trophic levels, producers, consumers (primary, secondary, tertiary, quaternary), decomposers, energy flow (Sun → chemical energy), carbon cycle (photosynthesis, respiration, feeding, decomposition, combustion, fossil fuels), nitrogen cycle (decomposition, nitrification, nitrogen fixation, denitrification, absorption), population growth (lag, exponential, stationary, death phases), limiting factors |
| 20 Human influences on ecosystems | food production (machinery, fertilisers, insecticides, herbicides, selective breeding, monocultures, intensive livestock); habitat destruction (deforestation, biodiversity loss, extinction, soil erosion, flooding, CO₂ increase); pollution (eutrophication, sewage, non-biodegradable plastics, methane, CO₂, enhanced greenhouse effect, climate change); conservation (sustainable resources, forests, fish stocks, endangered species, captive breeding, seed banks, artificial insemination, IVF) |
| 21 Biotechnology and genetic modification | bacteria (rapid reproduction, make complex molecules, plasmids), yeast (anaerobic respiration for ethanol/biofuels, bread-making), pectinase (fruit juice), biological washing powders (enzymes), lactase (lactose-free milk), fermenters (controlled conditions: temperature, pH, oxygen, nutrients, waste removal); genetic modification (restriction enzymes, DNA ligase, recombinant DNA, plasmids, transformation, examples: insulin production, herbicide/insect resistance, improved nutrition); advantages/disadvantages of GM crops |
