Metabolism is the word used to describe all the chemical reactions that happen within a cell or organism[reference:0]. Some reactions join smaller molecules together to make bigger ones (anabolism), while others break larger molecules into smaller ones (catabolism) to release energy.

## 1.1 Characteristics of Living Organisms (MRS GREN)

| Process | Definition (Mark Scheme) | Key Words | Common Trap |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| **Movement** | an action by an organism or part of an organism causing a change of position or place | action, change of position | Plant growth towards light is movement |
| **Respiration** | the chemical reactions in cells that break down nutrient molecules and release energy for metabolism | chemical reactions, release energy | ≠ breathing / gas exchange |
| **Sensitivity** | the ability to detect and respond to changes in the internal or external environment | detect, respond, stimuli | Also called irritability |
| **Growth** | a permanent increase in size and **dry mass** | permanent, dry mass | Seed swelling is NOT growth (dry mass unchanged) |
| **Reproduction** | the processes that make more of the same kind of organism | make more, same kind | Asexual vs sexual |
| **Excretion** | the removal of the waste products of **metabolism** and substances in excess of requirements | metabolic waste, removal | NOT egestion (undigested food) |
| **Nutrition** | the taking in of materials for energy, growth and development | taking in materials, energy | Plants are autotrophic but still nutrition |

> **Metabolism** = all chemical reactions within a cell/organism. Excretion removes metabolic waste (urea, CO₂) – otherwise they accumulate and interfere with normal metabolism.

## 1.2 Classification Systems

### Species
- **Definition**: a group of organisms that can reproduce to produce **fertile offspring**.
- **Example**: horse + donkey → mule (infertile) → horse and donkey are different species.

### Binomial Naming
- Genus capitalised, species lower case; *italicised* (underlined if handwritten).
- Example: *Homo sapiens*, *Panthera leo*.
- **Trap**: *Homo Sapiens* (wrong – species must not be capitalised).

### Dichotomous Key
- **Definition**: a series of **paired statements** used to identify organisms based on **observable features**.
- **Trap**: Do not confuse with phylogenetic tree (branching diagram for evolution) or classification (systematic arrangement by shared features).

### DNA & Evolutionary Relationships
- Classification aims to show **how closely related organisms are through evolution**.
- DNA base sequences: more similar → more recent common ancestor → closer relationship.
- Modern classification uses **DNA sequences** and **protein amino acid sequences** in addition to morphology/anatomy.

## 1.3 Features of Organisms

### Five Kingdoms

| Kingdom | Key Features | Examples |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **Animalia** | multicellular, heterotrophic, no cell wall | human, insect |
| **Plantae** | multicellular, autotrophic (photosynthesis), cell wall (cellulose) | moss, fern, flowering plant |
| **Fungi** | cell wall (**chitin**), saprotrophic, multicellular (except yeast) | mushroom, yeast |
| **Prokaryotae** | unicellular, **no true nucleus**, circular DNA | bacteria |
| **Protoctista** | mostly unicellular, have nucleus, some autotrophic some heterotrophic | Amoeba, algae |

### Viruses
- **Not considered living** – acellular, no metabolism, cannot reproduce outside a host.
- Have a **protein coat** and **genetic material (DNA or RNA)**.
- **Belong to no kingdom**.

### Vertebrate Classes (Detailed)

| Class | Body Covering | Respiration | Reproduction | Temperature | Unique Features |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| **Fish** | scales (mostly) | gills | external fertilisation, eggs in water | ectothermic | fins, lateral line |
| **Amphibians** | moist, smooth, permeable skin | gills (larva) → lungs + skin (adult) | eggs in water, jelly‑like | ectothermic | metamorphosis |
| **Reptiles** | dry, scaly skin | lungs | amniotic eggs (leathery/hard shell) on land | ectothermic | first fully terrestrial vertebrates |
| **Birds** | feathers | lungs + air sacs | hard‑shelled eggs | **endothermic** | hollow bones, beak |
| **Mammals** | hair / fur | **lungs** (with **diaphragm** – unique to mammals) | most give birth to live young (except monotremes) | **endothermic** | **diaphragm** muscle for breathing; mammary glands produce milk |

> **Diaphragm** is a sheet of muscle separating the thorax and abdomen; it contracts and flattens to draw air into the lungs. **Only mammals have a diaphragm** – this is a defining feature.

### Arthropod Classes

| Class | Legs | Antennae | Body parts | Examples |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| **Insects** | 3 pairs (6) | 1 pair | head, thorax, abdomen | beetle, bee |
| **Arachnids** | 4 pairs (8) | none | cephalothorax + abdomen | spider, scorpion |
| **Crustaceans** | many pairs (10+) | 2 pairs | often two main parts | crab, shrimp |
| **Myriapods** | many pairs (30+) | 1 pair | long segmented body | centipede, millipede |

### Plant Groups

#### Ferns (Pteridophytes)
- Reproduce by **spores** (not seeds).
- True roots, stems, leaves; **no cotyledons**.
- Example: *Dryopteris* (male fern).

#### Flowering Plants (Angiosperms)

| Feature | Monocotyledons | Dicotyledons |
|:---|:---|:---|
| Cotyledons | 1 | 2 |
| Leaf venation | **parallel** | **reticulate (netted)** |
| Root system | fibrous | tap root |
| Vascular bundles | scattered | arranged in a ring |
| Floral parts | multiples of 3 | multiples of 4 or 5 |
| Examples | maize, grass, lily | rose, bean, oak |

## Common Exam Traps (Paper 2)

| Wrong idea | Correct statement |
|:---|:---|
| Respiration = breathing | Respiration = chemical reactions in cells |
| Excretion = egestion | Excretion removes metabolic waste; egestion removes undigested food |
| Growth = increase in wet mass | Growth = permanent increase in **dry mass** |
| Viruses are prokaryotes | Viruses belong to **no kingdom** |
| Fungi have cellulose cell walls | Fungi have **chitin** cell walls |
| Bacteria have a true nucleus | Bacteria have **no true nucleus** |
| All arthropods have 6 legs | Only insects have 6 legs; arachnids 8, crustaceans many |
| All reptiles lay eggs | Some reptiles give birth to live young |
| All vertebrates have lungs | Fish have gills; amphibians also use skin |
