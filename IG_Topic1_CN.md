## 1.1 生物的特征 (Characteristics of Living Organisms)

### 1.1.1 代谢 (Metabolism) – 总览

**Metabolism** is the word used to describe **all** the chemical reactions that happen within a cell or organism[reference:0]. Some reactions join smaller molecules together to make bigger ones (anabolism), while others break larger molecules into smaller ones (catabolism) to release energy.

> **考场应对**：如果题目要求给代谢下定义，必须包含 **"all chemical reactions"** 和 **"in a cell or organism"** 这两个关键词，缺一不可。

### 1.1.2 七大生命过程 (MRS GREN) – 代谢视角

MRS GREN 中的每一个过程本质上是代谢的具体体现。下表给出了每个过程的 **标准定义（Mark Scheme 版本）** 及 **代谢联系**，可直接用于 Paper 4 简答题。

| 过程 | 标准定义（按 Mark Scheme） | 代谢联系（用于高分 Explain） | 考试陷阱（Paper 2） |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| **Movement** | an action by an organism or part of an organism causing a change of position or place | 运动所需的能量来自呼吸作用（代谢的一部分） | 植物的向光性生长也属于 movement（符合定义，尽管整体位置没变） |
| **Respiration** | **the chemical reactions in cells** that break down nutrient molecules and release energy for metabolism[reference:1] | 呼吸作用是**代谢的核心组成部分**，它产生的能量（ATP）驱动所有其他代谢反应 | 不是 breathing / gas exchange；Paper 2 常在此设陷阱 |
| **Sensitivity** | the ability to detect and respond to changes in the internal or external environment[reference:2] | 响应刺激涉及信号转导途径（一系列化学反应）和效应器的能量消耗 | 也叫 irritability |
| **Growth** | a permanent increase in size and **dry mass**[reference:3] | 生长需要合成新细胞物质，这些合成反应（蛋白质、核酸等）是代谢的一部分 | 种子吸水膨胀不是 **growth**（dry mass 未增，只有 water mass 增） |
| **Reproduction** | the processes that make more of the same kind of organism[reference:4] | 细胞分裂、配子形成等涉及 DNA 复制和蛋白质合成（代谢） | 无性与有性后代差异 |
| **Excretion** | the removal of the waste products of **metabolism** and substances in excess of requirements[reference:5] | **代谢废物**（urea, CO₂）必须被移除，否则会干扰其他代谢反应 | 不是 egestion（排遗） |
| **Nutrition** | the taking in of materials for energy, growth and development[reference:6] | 营养物质为代谢提供**原料**和**能量** | 植物自养也是 nutrition（自己制造有机分子也是“taking in materials”的广义理解） |

> **⭐ Paper 4 高分技巧**：如果题目问 "Explain why excretion is important"，你需要把 **"removal of waste products of metabolism"** 写出来，并说明如果不移除，这些废物会 **accumulate and interfere with normal metabolic reactions**。因果链越完整，得分越高。

### 1.1.3 Dry Mass vs. Wet Mass – 生长测量的唯一标准

- **Dry mass**：去除所有水分后的物质质量。
- **为什么是“干质量”而不是“湿质量”？** 水分含量会因环境变化而波动（例如雨后植物吸水），导致湿质量测量不准确。只有去除水分的干质量才能反映**真正的新物质合成**（生长）。
- **测量干质量的方法**：将样本在烤箱中烘干至恒重，再用天平称量。缺点是会杀死样本，因此通常只用于批量取样。
- 考试陷阱：如果选项中用 "increase in mass" 而不提 "dry" 或 "permanent"，大概率是错误选项；Paper 2 常在此设陷阱。

## 1.2 分类系统的概念与应用 (Concept and Uses of Classification Systems)

### 1.2.1 物种 (Species)

| 定义 | 关键词 | 例子 | 考试陷阱 |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| a group of organisms that can **reproduce** to produce **fertile offspring**[reference:7] | reproduce, fertile offspring | horse + donkey → mule (**infertile**) → 不同物种 | 马和驴不是同一物种；骡子 **infertile** 是关键判定依据 |

### 1.2.2 双名法 (Binomial Naming)

- **格式**：Genus 首字母大写 + species 全小写。印刷时**斜体**，手写时**加下划线**。
- **例子**：*Homo sapiens* (人类)，*Panthera leo* (狮子)[reference:8]。
- **常见错误**：写 *Homo Sapiens*（species 首字母大写）或 *Homo sapiens*（未斜体）。Paper 2 选择题常以 "Which is the correct way to write the binomial name?" 考查规则。
- **Genus 的定义**：The first part of a binomial name (e.g., *Panthera*). **All lions belong to the genus *Panthera***. 狮子不是 *Panthera leo* 的唯一物种，豹、虎等也属于 *Panthera* 属。

### 1.2.3 二分检索表 (Dichotomous Key)

- **定义**：a series of **paired statements** used to identify organisms based on observable features.
- **构造原则**：每对 statements 必须**互斥**（例如“有羽毛” vs “无羽毛”），且覆盖所有可能性。
- **考试常见**：根据给出的 key 和特征图，推算 organism 属于哪一组。做此类题时，从第一组开始逐条往下走，不要在中间跳跃。
- **重要辨析（Paper 2 高频陷阱）**：
  - 题目问 "Define a dichotomous key"，高分答案必须写出 **"based on observable features"**，只写 "a series of paired statements" 不够充分。
  - "a systematic arrangement of organisms according to shared features" → 这是 **classification** 的定义，不是 **dichotomous key** 的定义！Paper 2 曾将两者作为干扰选项并列，要求考生区分。
  - "a branching diagram used to show evolutionary relationships" → 这是 **phylogenetic tree**，不是 **dichotomous key**。这是 Paper 2 选择题高频混淆点。

### 1.2.4 分类与进化关系

- **分类是现代分类学的核心原则**：classification aims to show **how closely related organisms are through evolution**[reference:9]。
- **DNA 碱基序列**：亲缘关系越近，碱基序列相似度越高[reference:10]。
- **分子生物学证据的分类优势**：传统分类基于**形态学**（形状/结构）和**解剖学**[reference:11]；现代分类同时使用 **DNA 序列**和氨基酸序列，更精确地反映进化关系。即使是外貌相似但 DNA 不同的物种，也会被重新分类。
- **Paper 4 答题模板**：答案必须形成完整因果链——`groups share a more recent common ancestor → DNA base sequences more similar → classification reflects evolutionary relationships`。只写其中一句不完整。
- **分类层级**：**Kingdom, Phylum, Class, Order, Family, Genus, Species** (记忆口诀：Kings Play Chess On Fine Green Silk)。

## 1.3 生物的主要类群 (Features of Organisms)

### 1.3.1 五界系统 (Five Kingdoms)

| 界 | 主要特征 | 例子 |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **Animalia** | multicellular, **heterotrophic**, no cell wall, mostly move[reference:12] | human, insect, fish |
| **Plantae** | multicellular, **autotrophic** (photosynthesis), cell wall (**cellulose**)[reference:13] | moss, fern, flowering plant |
| **Fungi** | multicellular (except yeast), cell wall (**chitin**), **saprotrophic**[reference:14] | mushroom, mould, yeast |
| **Prokaryotae** | unicellular, **no true nucleus** (circular DNA), no membrane‑bound organelles[reference:15] | bacteria, archaea |
| **Protoctista** | mostly unicellular, have a nucleus, some autotrophic, some heterotrophic[reference:16] | Amoeba, Paramecium, algae |

**重要辨析（Paper 2 高频陷阱）**：
- 题目问 "Which feature is common to all members of the animal kingdom?" 高分答案是 **"heterotrophic"**（没有细胞壁、能够运动等并非所有动物共有，但所有动物都是异养的）。
- "Which kingdom has members with cell walls made of chitin?" → **Fungi**。
- "Which kingdom has members that are prokaryotic?" → **Prokaryotae**。
- 有机体可以属于多个界？**No**，每个物种只能属于一个界。

### 1.3.2 病毒 (Viruses)

- **为什么不是生物**：**not considered living**; 没有细胞结构，只有 **protein coat** 和 **genetic material (DNA or RNA)**; **reproduce only inside living host cells**[reference:17]。
- **不属于任何界**：acellular (非细胞形态), 不能独立代谢。
- **考题示例**："Which row correctly describes a virus?" Paper 2 干扰项可能包括 "has a nucleus", "can reproduce outside a host", "is prokaryotic"。
- **Paper 4 答题模板**：必须写出两个关键词：**"protein coat"** 和 **"reproduce only inside living host cells"**。只写一个不完整。

### 1.3.3 植物类群 (Plant Groups) – 详细版

#### 蕨类 (Ferns / Pteridophytes)

- **繁殖方式**：**spores** (孢子)，不是种子[reference:18]。
- **孢子位置**：在叶片背面，聚集在 **sori** 中，孢子囊 (sporangia) 产生和释放孢子[reference:19]。
- **结构**：有 **true roots, stems and leaves** (fronds)；有维管组织 (xylem and phloem)[reference:20]。
- **生长环境**：通常生长在潮湿、阴凉的地方。
- **生活史**：具有世代交替 (alternation of generations)，孢子体 (diploid) 和配子体 (haploid) 交替。
- **子叶数量 (cotyledons)**：**0** (ferns do not have cotyledons; they reproduce via spores and have a free-living gametophyte stage)[reference:21]。
- **叶脉类型 (leaf venation)**：通常为 **pinnate** (羽状) 或 dichotomous (二叉分叉)[reference:22]。
- **维管束排列**：有真正的 xylem 和 phloem；维管束形成 **loose ring**，但不如双子叶植物清晰[reference:23]。
- **根系类型**：从根茎 (rhizome) 生长出 **tap-root**[reference:24]。
- **花结构**：无花；在 sporangia 中产生孢子，sporangia 通常聚集成 sori 在 fronds 背面[reference:25]。
- **次生生长**：有限；部分蕨类有加厚的茎，但缺乏真正的次生木质部[reference:26]。
- **例子**：*Dryopteris filix-mas* (欧洲蕨) – 具有 sori 的典型 fronds[reference:27]。

#### 开花植物 (Flowering Plants / Angiosperms)

- **繁殖方式**：通过 **flowers → seeds** (种子)[reference:28]。
- **软木 vs 硬木**：考纲不要求区分，但知道某些双子叶植物可形成木本茎有助于理解次生生长的概念。
- **两大类**：**Monocotyledons (Monocots)** 和 **Dicotyledons (Dicots)**。

#### Monocots and Dicots – Comparison Table

| Feature | Ferns (Pteridophytes) | Monocotyledons (Monocots) | Dicotyledons (Dicots) |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| **Seeds / Spores** | Spores only[reference:29] | Seeds present[reference:30] | Seeds present[reference:31] |
| **Cotyledons** | 0 (free-living gametophyte, no cotyledon)[reference:32] | 1 (single seed leaf within the embryo)[reference:33] | 2 (two seed leaves)[reference:34] |
| **Leaf venation** | Pinnate or dichotomous[reference:35] | **Parallel** (veins run parallel along leaf length)[reference:36] | **Reticulate / Netted** (veins form branching network)[reference:37] |
| **Root system** | Tap-root arising from rhizome[reference:38] | **Fibrous** (many similarly sized roots from base)[reference:39] | **Tap-root** (one main root, smaller lateral roots)[reference:40] |
| **Vascular bundles** | Loose ring (less distinct)[reference:41] | **Scattered** (no distinct arrangement)[reference:42] | **Arranged in a ring** (distinct)[reference:43] |
| **Floral parts** | None (spores)[reference:44] | **Multiples of 3**[reference:45] | **Multiples of 4 or 5**[reference:46] |
| **Secondary growth** | Limited / absent[reference:47] | Generally absent (mostly herbaceous)[reference:48] | Usually present (most woody plants)[reference:49] |
| **Examples** | Dryopteris filix-mas (male fern)[reference:50] | Zea mays (maize) / grasses, lilies, orchids, palms[reference:51] | Rosa spp. (rose) / beans, oaks, sunflowers[reference:52] |

### 1.3.4 脊椎动物五大纲 (Vertebrate Classes) – 详细版

#### 通用定义

- **Vertebrates**：animals with a **backbone (spinal column)**[reference:53]。共有 **5 classes**：fish, amphibians, reptiles, birds, mammals[reference:54]。
- **Vertebrates 的共有特征**：Endoskeleton (bone or cartilage), dorsal hollow nerve cord, closed circulatory system with multi‑chambered heart, true coelom, paired appendages (fins or limbs), well‑developed brain[reference:55]。

#### Detailed Features of Each Class

| Class | Body Covering | Respiration | Reproduction | Temperature Regulation | Other Key Features |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| **Fish** | **Scales** (most species), streamlined body[reference:56] | **Gills** throughout life[reference:57] | External fertilization (spawning); lay eggs (no shell) in water[reference:58] | **Ectothermic** (cold‑blooded, body temperature varies with environment)[reference:59] | Fins for movement; lateral line system for detecting vibrations[reference:60] |
| **Amphibians** | **Moist, smooth, permeable skin** (cutaneous respiration)[reference:61] | **Lungs + skin** (as adults); **gills** (as larvae / tadpoles) | External fertilization; lay eggs in water (jelly‑like, no shell)[reference:62] | **Ectothermic** | Undergo **metamorphosis** (tadpole → adult); dual life cycle (aquatic larva, terrestrial adult)[reference:63] |
| **Reptiles** | **Dry, scaly skin** (scales made of keratin)[reference:64] | **Lungs** throughout life | Internal fertilization; lay **amniotic eggs** with leathery/hard shells (evolved to prevent water loss, adapt to dry land)[reference:65] | **Ectothermic**[reference:66] | Eggs can develop on land; some species give birth to live young (e.g., certain snakes)[reference:67] |
| **Birds** | **Feathers** (warmth and flight); beak (no teeth)[reference:68] | **Lungs** + **air sacs** (enhanced efficiency) | Internal fertilization; lay **hard‑shelled eggs** (calcareous)[reference:69] | **Endothermic** (warm‑blooded, constant internal temperature) | **Lightweight hollow bones** for flight[reference:70] |
| **Mammals** | **Hair or fur** (insulation)[reference:71] | **Lungs** | Internal fertilization; most give birth to **live young** (exception: monotremes lay eggs)[reference:72] | **Endothermic**[reference:73] | **Mammary glands** produce milk to feed young[reference:74] |

#### 高等考点 (Extended Level – 进化适应性)

| Class | Key Adaptations for Exam‑level Explanations |
|:---|:---|
| **Fish** | Streamlined body reduces water resistance during swimming; gills maximize oxygen extraction from water; lateral line system detects movement/vibration[reference:75] |
| **Amphibians** | Moist permeable skin allows cutaneous respiration (supplements lungs); require water for reproduction; metamorphosis allows larval stage to exploit aquatic resources and adult stage to exploit terrestrial resources[reference:76] |
| **Reptiles** | Amniotic egg (leathery/hard shell) allows reproduction on dry land without returning to water; dry scaly skin prevents water loss in terrestrial environments[reference:77] |
| **Birds** | Feathers for insulation and flight; hollow bones reduce body weight for flight efficiency; endothermy maintains high metabolic rate for sustained flight |
| **Mammals** | Hair/fur for insulation in cold environments; endothermy ensures stable body temperature independent of environment; mammary glands allow offspring to be nourished after birth, reducing dependence on immediate environmental food sources |

#### ⚠️ 常见考试陷阱（Paper 2 选择题）

| 题干/选项 | 正误 | 解析 |
|:---|:---|:---|
| "All fish have scales." | ❌ | 某些鱼（如 catfish）没有鳞片。Paper 2 曾在选项中用 "all" 设陷阱 |
| "All birds can fly." | ❌ | 鸵鸟 (ostrich)、企鹅 (penguin) 等不能飞 |
| "Amphibians rely only on lungs for gas exchange." | ❌ | 两栖动物的皮肤是重要的气体交换器官（cutaneous respiration） |
| "Reptiles’ skin is moist." | ❌ | 爬行动物的皮肤是干燥的，有鳞片 |
| "Mammals are the only vertebrates with hair." | ✅ | 哺乳动物是唯一有毛发/皮毛的脊椎动物 |

### 1.3.5 节肢动物四大纲 (Arthropod Classes) – 详细版

#### 节肢动物的共有特征

- **Exoskeleton** of chitin (must be shed for growth: ecdysis/molting)[reference:78]
- **Segmented body**, jointed appendages[reference:79]
- **Open circulatory system** (hemolymph pumped into body cavity)[reference:80]
- **Ventral nerve cord**[reference:81]

| Class | Body Structure | Number of Legs | Antennae | Other Key Features | Examples |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| **Myriapods** (多足类) | Long, **segmented body** (each segment with 1‑2 pairs of legs)[reference:82] | Many pairs (from 30 up) | 1 pair[reference:83] | Numerous legs, slow movement (millipedes, herbivorous; centipedes, carnivorous with venomous claws) | centipede, millipede |
| **Insects** (昆虫) | Three distinct body regions: **head, thorax, abdomen**[reference:84] | **3 pairs (6 legs)**, attached to thorax[reference:85] | 1 pair[reference:86] | Usually **2 pairs of wings** (attached to thorax); body covered by exoskeleton; many undergo metamorphosis[reference:87] | beetle, bee, butterfly |
| **Arachnids** (蛛形纲) | Two body regions: **cephalothorax and abdomen**[reference:88] | **4 pairs (8 legs)**[reference:89] | **None**[reference:90] | No wings; simple eyes; many produce silk or venom；chelicerae (mouthparts) for feeding | spider, scorpion, tick |
| **Crustaceans** (甲壳纲) | Often two main body parts (cephalothorax + abdomen)[reference:91] | Many pairs (usually 10+; first pair may be modified as claws)[reference:92] | **2 pairs**[reference:93] | Primarily **aquatic** (water‑living); have **gills**; often covered by hard **carapace**[reference:94] | crab, lobster, shrimp |

#### ⚠️ 节肢动物高频考点与陷阱（Paper 2 选择题）

| 题干/选项 | 正误 | 解析 |
|:---|:---|:---|
| "All insects have wings." | ❌ | 部分昆虫（如 ants、worker termites）无翅膀 |
| "All arthropods have antennae." | ❌ | 蛛形纲没有触角 |
| "All arthropods have 6 legs." | ❌ | 只有昆虫是 3 pairs (6 legs) |
| "All arthropods have an exoskeleton." | ✅ | 所有节肢动物都有外骨骼，这是它们的共有特征 |
| "Crustaceans always live in water." | ❌ | 大部分生活在水中，但有些（如 woodlice）生活在潮湿的陆地环境中 |
| "Myriapods include centipedes and millipedes." | ✅ | 多足类包括蜈蚣（centipedes）和马陆（millipedes） |

## 📌 考试常见陷阱汇总（Paper 2 选择题）

| 错误观念 | 正确说法 | 对应 Sub‑topic |
|:---|:---|:---|
| Respiration = breathing | Respiration is chemical reactions in cells | 1.1 |
| Excretion = egestion | Excretion = metabolic waste; egestion = undigested food | 1.1 |
| Growth = increase in wet mass | Growth = permanent increase in **dry mass** | 1.1 |
| Viruses are prokaryotes | Viruses are **not classified in any kingdom** | 1.2 |
| Dichotomous key = a branching diagram for evolution | Dichotomous key = a **series of paired statements** for identification | 1.2 |
| Classification based only on morphology | Classification uses **DNA, proteins, and morphology** | 1.2 |
| Fungi have cellulose cell walls | Fungi have **chitin** cell walls | 1.3 |
| Bacteria have a true nucleus | Bacteria (prokaryotes) have **no true nucleus**, DNA is circular and free in the cytoplasm | 1.3 |
| Mule is a species | Horse and donkey are **different species** (mule is infertile) | 1.2 |
| Binomial name as *Homo Sapiens* | Genus capitalised, species lower case: ***Homo sapiens*** (italicised or underlined) | 1.2 |
| All arthropods have 6 legs | Insects 6 legs; arachnids 8; crustaceans many pairs | 1.3 |
| All plants reproduce by seeds | Ferns reproduce by **spores** | 1.3 |
| All vertebrates have lungs | Fish have **gills**; some amphibians use **skin** | 1.3 |
| All reptiles lay eggs | Some reptiles (e.g., certain snakes and lizards) give birth to **live young** | 1.3 |
