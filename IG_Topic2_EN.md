## 2.1 Cell Structure

### 2.1.1 Animal and Plant Cells

| Organelle | Function | Present in both? | Key exam point |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| **Cell membrane** | controls movement of substances in/out; partially permeable | ✅ | ~7–8 nm thick; phospholipid bilayer + proteins |
| **Nucleus** | contains DNA, controls cell activities | ✅ | nuclear pores allow mRNA to pass |
| **Cytoplasm** | site of chemical reactions; contains organelles | ✅ | many metabolic reactions occur here (e.g. glycolysis) |
| **Mitochondria** | site of **aerobic respiration**, produces ATP | ✅ | double membrane; inner membrane folded into cristae |
| **Ribosomes** | site of **protein synthesis** | ✅ | made of rRNA + protein; free or attached to RER |
| **Cell wall** | provides structural support; made of **cellulose** | ❌ (plant only) | fully permeable; plant cell wall = cellulose; bacterial cell wall different |
| **Chloroplasts** | site of **photosynthesis**; contain chlorophyll | ❌ (**only green parts**) | **Not all plant cells have them** (e.g. root cells, epidermal cells usually lack chloroplasts) |
| **Large vacuole** | stores water, salts, sugars; helps maintain turgor pressure | ❌ (plant only) | animal cells may have small vacuoles (e.g. phagocytic vesicles), but not a large central vacuole |

**Comparison table (common exam question)**

| Feature | Animal cell | Plant cell |
|:---|:---|:---|
| Cell wall | ❌ | ✅ (cellulose) |
| Chloroplasts | ❌ | ✅ (**only green parts**, e.g. mesophyll, guard cells) |
| Large vacuole | ❌ | ✅ |
| Shape | irregular / rounded | fixed / polygonal (due to cell wall) |
| Storage carbohydrate | glycogen | starch |

> **⭐ Paper 4 template**: If asked “State two structures present in plant cells but absent in animal cells”, give **cell wall**, **chloroplast**, **large vacuole** (any two). But remember chloroplasts are only found in photosynthetic parts.

### 2.1.2 Specialised Cells

#### Animal cells

| Cell type | Adaptations | Function | Link to Topic 1 |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| **Red blood cell** | biconcave disc (↑ SA:vol), no nucleus (more space for haemoglobin), contains haemoglobin | transport oxygen | Topic 9 (circulation) |
| **Sperm cell** | flagellum (tail) for movement; midpiece with many mitochondria (energy); acrosome (enzymes to penetrate egg) | fertilisation | Topic 16 (reproduction) |
| **Neurone** | long axon (transmits impulses), branched dendrites (receive signals), myelin sheath (insulation, faster conduction) | transmit nerve impulses | Topic 14 (coordination) |
| **Muscle cell** | many mitochondria (energy for contraction); actin & myosin filaments | movement | Topic 1 (movement) |
| **Phagocyte** | can change shape to engulf pathogens; contains lysosomes (digestive enzymes) | engulf and digest pathogens | Topic 10 (immunity) |
| **Ciliated epithelial cell** | has **cilia** (tiny hair‑like structures) that beat rhythmically | move mucus (in airways) or egg cells (in oviducts) | Topic 11 (gas exchange), Topic 16 (reproduction) |

#### Plant cells

| Cell type | Adaptations | Function | Link to Topic 1 |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| **Root hair cell** | long projection (↑ SA), many mitochondria (energy for active transport) | absorb water and mineral ions from soil | Topic 8 (plant transport) |
| **Palisade mesophyll** | many chloroplasts (near upper surface), cylindrical, tightly packed | photosynthesis (capture maximum light) | Topic 6 (plant nutrition) |
| **Spongy mesophyll** | irregular shape, air spaces (allow gas diffusion), fewer chloroplasts | gas exchange (CO₂ in, O₂ out) | Topics 6 & 11 |
| **Guard cell** | contains chloroplasts, unevenly thickened cell walls (thicker on stomatal side) | open/close stomata to control gas exchange and water loss | Topic 8 (transpiration) |
| **Sieve tube element** | no nucleus, few organelles, end walls with sieve plates | translocate sucrose and amino acids (phloem) | Topic 8 |
| **Xylem vessel** | dead, hollow, no end walls, lignin‑thickened walls | transport water and minerals (xylem); support | Topic 8 |

> **⚠️ Trap**: Root hair cells have **no chloroplasts** (they are underground), but they have **many mitochondria** because active transport of ions requires a lot of energy.

## 2.2 Bacterial Cell – Supplement (Extended)

Bacteria belong to kingdom **Prokaryotae**. Their structure differs from eukaryotes (animals, plants, fungi).

| Structure | Function | Notes |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **Cell wall** | shape, protection | made of **peptidoglycan** (not cellulose) |
| **Cell membrane** | controls movement | similar to eukaryotes, but no membrane‑bound organelles |
| **Cytoplasm** | contains ribosomes, enzymes | no cytoskeleton |
| **Ribosomes** | protein synthesis | smaller than eukaryotes (70S vs 80S) |
| **Circular DNA** | main genetic information | **free in cytoplasm** – no nuclear membrane |
| **Plasmid** | carries extra genes (e.g. antibiotic resistance); **used in genetic engineering** | small circular DNA, can replicate and transfer between bacteria |
| **Flagellum** | movement (some bacteria) | structure different from eukaryotic flagella |
| **Capsule** | protection, attachment | present in some bacteria; polysaccharide layer |

**Comparison: bacteria vs animal/plant cells (Paper 2 common)**

| Feature | Animal / plant cells | Bacterial cells |
|:---|:---|:---|
| Nucleus | true (membrane‑bound) | none (circular DNA free) |
| Membrane‑bound organelles (mitochondria, chloroplasts etc.) | yes | no |
| Ribosomes | 80S | 70S |
| Cell wall (if present) | cellulose (plant) or chitin (fungus) | peptidoglycan |
| DNA structure | linear DNA (on chromosomes) | circular DNA + plasmids |
| Size | 10–100 µm | 0.5–5 µm |

## 2.3 Cell Differentiation & Levels of Organisation

### 2.3.1 Differentiation

- **Definition**: the process by which a cell becomes specialised to perform a specific function.
- **Stem cell**: an undifferentiated cell that can divide to produce more stem cells or differentiate into specialised cell types.
- (Molecular mechanisms involving gene expression belong to Topic 17 – genetics – not detailed here.)
- **Animal stem cell example**: haematopoietic stem cells in bone marrow → red blood cells, white blood cells.
- **Plant stem cell example**: meristem tissue (shoot and root tips) – continuously divides and differentiates.

### 2.3.2 Levels of Organisation

| Level | Definition | IGCSE example |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **Cell** | basic structural and functional unit | muscle cell, red blood cell |
| **Tissue** | a group of similar cells working together to perform a specific function | muscle tissue, xylem tissue, phloem tissue |
| **Organ** | a group of different tissues working together | heart, leaf, stomach |
| **Organ system** | a group of organs working together | circulatory system, digestive system |
| **Organism** | a living individual composed of organ systems | human, frog, oak tree |

**⚠️ Common confusions**:
- **Phloem** is a **tissue** (not an organ).
- **Leaf** is an **organ** because it contains multiple tissues (epidermis, mesophyll, vascular bundles).
- **Circulatory system** is a **system** – includes heart (organ), blood vessels (organs), blood (tissue).

## 2.4 Microscopy & Measurement

### 2.4.1 Light Microscope vs Electron Microscope

| Feature | Light microscope | Electron microscope |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **Radiation source** | visible light | electron beam |
| **Resolution** | ~200 nm | ~0.1 nm (TEM) |
| **Maximum magnification** | ~1500× | up to 2,000,000× |
| **Specimen** | can observe living cells | must be fixed, dehydrated, sectioned – **cannot observe living cells** |
| **Image** | colour (stains) | black & white (greyscale) |
| **Use** | observe cells, tissues, small organisms | observe ultrastructure (e.g. cristae, ribosomes, viruses) |
| **Exam focus** | identify nucleus, chloroplasts, etc. | identify finer organelles (ribosomes, double membrane) |

### 2.4.2 Using a Microscope & Calculations

- **Total magnification** = eyepiece magnification × objective magnification.
- **Formulae**:
  - `Magnification = observed (image) size ÷ actual size` (units must be the same)
  - `actual size = observed size ÷ magnification`
- **Using a scale bar**: If the image has a scale bar (e.g. “10 µm”), measure the bar length with a ruler and use proportions to calculate actual size – no need to know magnification.
- **Calibrating an eyepiece graticule (Extended)**:
  1. Place a stage micrometer (known division length) on the stage.
  2. Align the two scales and calculate how many µm each eyepiece division corresponds to.
  3. Remove the stage micrometer, use the eyepiece graticule to measure the specimen, then multiply by the calibration factor.

**Exam tip**: Always show the formula and working when calculating magnification or actual size. Give final answer in micrometres (µm) if appropriate.

## 📌 Common Exam Traps (Paper 2)

| Wrong idea | Correct statement | Sub‑topic |
|:---|:---|:---|
| All plant cells have chloroplasts | Only green parts (e.g. leaves) have chloroplasts – root cells have none | 2.1 |
| Bacteria have a true nucleus | Bacteria are prokaryotes – no nuclear membrane, DNA free in cytoplasm | 2.2 |
| All cell walls have the same composition | Plant cell wall = cellulose; bacterial cell wall = peptidoglycan | 2.1 & 2.2 |
| Animal cells have a cell wall | ❌ Animal cells do not have a cell wall | 2.1 |
| Mitochondria are the site of photosynthesis | Mitochondria = **aerobic respiration**; chloroplasts = photosynthesis | 2.1 |
| Ribosomes have a membrane | Ribosomes are non‑membranous | 2.1 |
| Red blood cells have a nucleus | Mature mammalian red blood cells have **no nucleus** | 2.1 |
| Plants store energy as glycogen | Plants store **starch**; animals store **glycogen** | 2.1 |
| Sieve tube elements have a nucleus | Mature sieve tube elements have no nucleus – they are supported by companion cells | 2.3 |
| Electron microscopes can observe living cells | Electron microscopes require fixed, dehydrated specimens – **cannot observe living cells** | 2.4 |
